IR - Infrared Transmitting and Receiving

An infra-red transmitter and receiver allows an Arduino microcontroller to communicate with another arduino (which has an ir receiver)


To receive an ir pulse the Arduino needs to sample the data pin quickly.
A timer interrupt is created which allows the Arduino to read the data pin at a 50 microsecond rate.
That is 20000 times a second.
A pulse is considered "long" when it is on for 750 microseconds or longer a pulse is considerd "short" when it is less than 750 microseconds in duration
The long pulses are viewed as 1 and short pulses are viewed as 0.
In between each light pulse is an "off" or darkness which usually is always the same length of time (about 1 millisecond). Putting all the 1's and 0's together gives you the data value. For example 1000 represents the value 8. 1101 represents 13. These 1's and zeroes are binary numbers.
